Anonymous

I have been studying the “hacktivist” Anonymous movement since 2012. My interested is driven by the desire to understand new forms of global, decentralized collective action.

How can ethnically, religiously distinct individuals in each their own part of the globe unite in a shared cause without ever meeting offline? How can intense feelings of solidarity come forth in virtual chat-forums and faceless skype calls? I elaborate on these topics in my qualitative bachelor thesis (2012) which is available in Danish

here.

Currently I am researching how the state-of-the-art technology can answer previously unanswered questions. In my latest project I use Facebook data and social network analysis to illustrate the vastness and global interconnectivity of the movement. The graph below is an extract that illustrates Facebook pages affiliated with the movement and their connections through likes.
